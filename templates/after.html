Seek the advice and attention of a qualified healthcare professional for proper evaluation, diagnosis, and treatment.


Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects the way the body processes blood sugar (glucose), which is the primary source of energy for cells. The hormone insulin, produced by the pancreas, regulates the amount of glucose in the blood. In people with diabetes, either the pancreas does not produce enough insulin or the body cannot effectively use the insulin it produces. As a result, glucose builds up in the blood, which can lead to a range of complications. There are several types of diabetes

Causes

  • Genetic Predisposition: Family history can increase risk.
  • Weight and Obesity: Excess weight is a major risk factor.
  • Insulin Resistance: Cells become resistant to insulin.
  • Inactivity: Sedentary lifestyle promotes risk.
  • Poor Diet Choices: High sugar intake and processed foods.
  • Gestational Diabetes: During pregnancy increases risk.

Remedies

  • Choose a balanced and nutritious diet.
  • Engage in regular physical activity.
  • Aim for a healthy weight and body composition.
  • Practice stress management and prioritize self-care.
  • Limit alcohol intake, if consumed.
  • Monitor blood sugar levels consistently.
  • Include fiber-rich foods in your diet to help manage blood sugar levels.
